1031 Exchange / Escrow

Hire a Qualified Intermediary ("QI") or Escrow Attorney to hold funds in a separate account.

Properly comply with 1031 exchange rules or protect the integrity of your deal with a neutral, third party lawyer.

When do you need an escrow attorney?

As a custodian of funds

Intermediary control of a deposit of funds complies with specific legal or contractual requirements and helps mitigate conflicts of interest

As a legal safety net

Ensures that all the terms of a business or property sale/purchase are fully met before payment is released and protects against possible fraud.

Qualified Intermediary

A designated holder of real estate transactions to fully comply with IRS 1031 exchange rules

Our flat fee legal services as a Qualified Intermediary or Escrow Attorney includes at least the following:


  • Questionnaire to determine whether real property qualifies for 1031 exchange treatment
  • Explanation of 1031 exchange time limitations and property replacement rules
  • Preparation, review and execution of a Qualified Intermediary agreement
  • Creation of a separate, interest bearing escrow account for deposit of 1031 funds
  • Complimentary tracking and reminder of 45 day identification & 180 day completion period
  • Transfer of 1031 funds for purchase of replacement property as instructed per transaction
  • Final 1031 closing packet of all required exchange documents and escrow services rendered
